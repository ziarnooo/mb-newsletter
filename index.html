The Anti-Career Talk

Gray Skinner at Montgomery Bell Academy Career Day

Last month, I returned to my alma mater, Montgomery Bell Academy, for Career Day.

I told the boys something counterintuitive: I don’t really believe in “careers” - at least not the linear version where you choose a path at 18 and follow it for life.

Over time, one question has mattered more than any career plan:

What am I actually solving for? Not “what job should I choose?” but: what am I uniquely good at, and what truly matters to me?

The most fulfilled people I know didn’t execute perfect plans. They aligned how they spend their time with their values and strengths.
